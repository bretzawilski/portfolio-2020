Administration and Instructional Design

I've been fortunate to serve in a number of roles that have allowed me to engage with instructional design and the development of critical and rhetorical digital literacies. Technology and the humanities are intricately linked, and even in administrative positions, an awareness of writing as always technologically mediated informs my practice.

The following projects and roles describe further describe my committment to XYZ:

Coordinator of the Critical Skills Digital Symposium

The Digital Symposium is an end-of-year capstone project intended to ask students to draw upon the critical literacies they have developed throughout their two semesters of enrollment in Critical Skills. During the Digital Symposiums first implementation in 2020, the Covid-19 crisis resulted in a sudden shift to remote learning. This shift resulted in the creation of significant online resources intended to help students successfully develop an audio podcast building upon previous research they had gathered.

Additional details can be found on the Critical Skills Podcast Resource Page.

Responsibilities:

  • Developed initial project descriptor and scaffolding
  • Created detailed screencast videos for the following topics:
    • Introduction to the Project and Remote Learning
    • An Overview of the Medium of Podcasts
    • How to Record Audio on a Smarphone or Computer
    • How to Develop a Podcast Prototype for Early Critique
    • Techniques for Editing Recorded Audio in GarageBand, Audacity, or through Anchor
    • How to Build an Accompanying Web Page for a Podcast
    • How to Finalize and Circulate a Podcast

  • Trained Critical Skills instructors to evaluate, assess, and critique project submissions
  • Developed and implemented project rubric
  • Collected and circulated exemplar podcasts on a Student Showcase page
